1\. Launch Messaging Principles

Core launch message  
Spotsylvania RFC is a rugby club in formation. The first youth focus is non-contact tag rugby for elementary and middle school students, with a school-to-summer pathway through PE introductions, family interest, and summer play opportunities if there is enough support.

Primary call to action  
Join the Founding List

Campaign phrase  
Put Your Hand Up

Supporting phrase  
A club begins when enough people decide to put their hand up.

Core identities to use  
\- Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club  
\- Spotsylvania RFC  
\- Spotsylvania Youth Rugby  
\- Spotsylvania Tag Rugby

Use carefully  
\- Spotsy Rugby — optional youth/social shorthand only  
\- SPLV — secondary graphic code only  
\- Forged in Spotsylvania — secondary heritage/campaign phrase only

Avoid overclaiming  
Do not claim:  
\- the club is officially chartered unless confirmed  
\- the club is fully insured unless confirmed  
\- fields are secured unless confirmed  
\- schools are officially partnered unless confirmed  
\- a summer league is guaranteed unless confirmed  
\- Rugby Virginia / USA Rugby has approved a specific program unless confirmed

Use:  
\- in formation  
\- gauging interest  
\- exploring  
\- hoping to offer  
\- if there is enough interest  
\- subject to approvals, field access, insurance requirements, and volunteer support

3\. Ten-Post Launch Sequence

Sequence purpose  
The first launch sequence should explain the club clearly and invite action without sounding like a finished organization before it is ready.

Recommended rhythm:  
\- 2–3 posts per week for 3–4 weeks  
\- Repeat the founding list CTA often  
\- Keep posts clear and parent-friendly  
\- Mix youth, volunteer, adult, sponsor, and community messaging

Post 1 — Club in Formation  
Caption  
Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club is in formation.

We are gauging interest from parents, youth players, adult players, coaches, referees, volunteers, sponsors, teachers, and supporters who want to help bring rugby to Spotsylvania.

No commitment required. No experience required. Just put your hand up and help us determine whether the club has the people to begin.

Unity · Courage · Service  
Est. 2026

Join the Founding List.

Visual direction  
Use the primary crest on navy background. Keep it formal and simple.

Join the Founding List

Post 2 — Youth Tag Rugby  
Caption  
Our first youth focus is non-contact tag rugby for elementary and middle school students.

Spotsylvania Tag Rugby will introduce young players to the game through running, passing, spacing, support, evasion, teamwork, and fun.

No tackling. No scrums. No rucks. No mauls.

Just a safe, active first step into rugby.

Join the youth rugby interest list.

Post 4 — Parent Safety Message  
Caption  
Parents usually ask the right question first: Is this tackle rugby?

For the first youth offering, the answer is no.

The starting format is non-contact tag rugby. That means no tackling, scrums, rucks, mauls, or contact drills.

Players learn movement, passing, spacing, communication, teamwork, and confidence in an age-appropriate format.

Join the interest list to receive updates.

Visual direction  
Use calm, parent-friendly copy over cream or navy background.

CTA  
Learn More / Join the Founding List

8\. Launch Checklist

Before public launch  
\- Confirm website form works.  
\- Confirm local crest loads correctly.  
\- Confirm youth tag rugby section is live.  
\- Confirm Parent FAQ exists or is ready.  
\- Confirm no unverified claims about charter, insurance, schools, or fields.  
\- Confirm contact email is ready.  
\- Confirm social account names.  
\- Prepare first 3–5 posts.  
\- Prepare sponsor/supporter email.  
\- Prepare volunteer response workflow.

After first launch posts  
\- Track form submissions.  
\- Categorize by parent/player/coach/volunteer/sponsor/supporter.  
\- Identify possible founding adults.  
\- Identify possible PE teacher contacts.  
\- Identify possible sponsor leads.  
\- Send welcome email.  
\- Prepare interest meeting if response is strong.
