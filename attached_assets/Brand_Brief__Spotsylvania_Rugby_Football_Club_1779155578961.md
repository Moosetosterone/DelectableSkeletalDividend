# **Brand Brief: Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club**

## **1\. Brand Name**

**Formal name:** Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club  
**Common name:** Spotsylvania RFC  
**Monogram:** SRFC

**SRFC** should be the primary abbreviation across badges, kits, apparel, registration materials, and social media. It is clearer and more traditional than “SRC.”

---

## **2\. Brand Essence**

**Spotsylvania RFC is a county-rooted youth rugby club that forms young people in character, courage, discipline, and service through the game of rugby.**

Rugby is the means. Formation is the end.

The brand should feel like a lasting local institution, not a seasonal youth sports team.

---

## **3\. Core Brand Idea**

**Formed Through Rugby**

This remains the strongest public-facing idea. It is clear, accessible, and mission-driven without being overtly religious.

Secondary brand line:

**One Club. Three Paths. United in Purpose.**

This now fits the architecture beautifully.

---

## **4\. Positioning Statement**

**Spotsylvania RFC is a traditional, family-friendly youth rugby club serving Spotsylvania County, Virginia. The club uses rugby to form young players in courage, discipline, humility, teamwork, resilience, and service while building a welcoming community rooted in local heritage.**

---

## **5\. Brand Personality**

The club should feel:

**Traditional** — rooted in rugby and heraldic club language  
**Local** — clearly connected to Spotsylvania County  
**Disciplined** — structured, serious, and well-coached  
**Welcoming** — family-friendly, accessible, and not elitist  
**Rugged** — physical, resilient, and honest  
**Virtuous** — grounded in courage, humility, service, responsibility, and respect  
**Formational** — focused on developing young people, not merely winning  
**Heritage-minded** — respectful of local symbolism and tradition  
**Community-rooted** — built for families, players, coaches, schools, and local supporters

Avoid anything gimmicky, flashy, cartoonish, esports-like, corporate, overly aggressive, militaristic for its own sake, or overtly religious.

---

## **6\. Brand Architecture**

Spotsylvania RFC should be understood as **one club with multiple sides, age grades, formats, and developmental stages**.

### **Master Institution**

**Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club**

The club is the unifying culture-carrier. It owns the mission, values, player formation, coaching standards, colors, formal crest, and code of conduct.

### **Formal Club Crest**

Used for:

* Website  
* Official club documents  
* Letterhead  
* Certificates  
* Sponsor materials  
* Awards  
* Banners  
* Founder/board materials

The formal crest is the “constitutional symbol” of the club.

### **Playing Marks**

Used for:

* Jerseys  
* Shorts  
* Training tops  
* Polos  
* Hats  
* Team apparel  
* Social graphics  
* Age-level communications

The simplified marks represent developmental stages within the club.

---

## **7\. Identity System: One Club, Three Levels**

### **Formal Club Crest**

**Use:** Website and official materials

**Elements:**

* Traditional shield  
* Forest green outer border  
* Antique gold inner border  
* Cream/ivory field  
* Burgundy chevron  
* Central stag head  
* Oak leaf/acorn motif  
* Anvil  
* Cannon wheel  
* Motto ribbon: **Patior ut Potiar**  
* Founding year: **Est. 2026**, assuming 2026 remains the correct year

**Meaning:**  
The formal crest represents the full institution: local heritage, growth, formation, strength, and controlled courage.

### **Level 1: SRFC Stags**

**Badge:** SRFC \+ Stag  
**Use:** Touch / tag / flag rugby teams

**Meaning:**  
The Stags represent the entry point into the club: movement, belonging, growth, confidence, and love of the game.

Possible informal younger group:  
**SRFC Young Bucks** for pre-U8 or introductory players.

### **Level 2: SRFC Forge**

**Badge:** SRFC \+ Hammer and Anvill  
**Use:** JV tackle rugby teams

**Meaning:**  
The Forge represents the move into more serious contact rugby: discipline, pressure, refinement, resilience, strength, and leadership.

The hammer-and-anvil mark communicates that varsity players are not merely playing harder rugby; they are being shaped into stronger young men.

### **Level 3: SRFC Cannons**

**Badge:** SRFC \+ Cannon Wheel  
**Use:** Varsity tackle rugby teams

**Meaning:**  
The Cannons represent mature formation: discipline, pressure, refinement, resilience, strength, and leadership.

The cannon wheel is preferable to a firing cannon because it feels historical and restrained rather than aggressive.

## **8\. Naming System**

Recommended structure:

**SRFC \[Level / Team Identity\] \[Age or Format\]**

Examples:

* **SRFC Young Bucks U6 & below**  
* **SRFC Stags U8**  
* **SRFC Stags U10 Flag**  
* **SRFC Stags Touch Rugby**  
* **SRFC Forge JV XV**  
* **SRFC Forge 7s**  
* **SRFC Cannons Varsity XV**  
* **SRFC Cannons Varsity 7s**

For public-facing simplicity:

* **Touch / Tag / Flag:** SRFC Stags  
* **JV Tackle:** SRFC Forge  
* **Varsity Tackle:** SRFC Cannons

## **10\. Motto and Language**

### **Primary public-facing idea**

**Formed Through Rugby**

### **System line**

**One Club. Three Paths. United in Purpose.**

### **Formal motto**

**Patior ut Potiar**  
“I suffer in order to obtain.” OR “I suffer to attain.”

Use this with restraint. It works best on:

* Formal crest  
* Website About page  
* Founder materials  
* Awards  
* Internal culture pieces  
* Back-neck detail on heritage apparel

Avoid making the Latin motto the dominant public-facing phrase for younger families. Let **Formed Through Rugby** do the public-facing work.

* Responsibility  
* Resilience  
* Honor  
* Stewardship

* Teamwork  
* Controlled strength

Avoid:

* Destroy  
* Crush  
* Dominate  
* Savage  
* War

* Fearless warriors

  ## **11\. Color Palette**

The SRFC color system is:

### **Forest Green**

**HEX:** `#1E3D2E`  
Primary club color. Represents county roots, fields, woodland, growth, discipline, and tradition.

### **Cream / Ivory**

**HEX:** `#F6F2E6`  
Base color. Adds warmth, tradition, family-friendliness, and an old-club feel.

### **Antique Gold**

**HEX:** `#B89A4A`  
Accent color. Represents honor, heritage, excellence, and civic dignity.

### **Heritage Burgundy**

**HEX:** `#7A1F28`  
Heritage accent and alternate kit color. Represents courage, sacrifice, local heraldic tradition, and match-day strength.

### **Color logic**

**Green** is the club.  
**Cream** is the tradition and warmth.  
**Gold** is the honor and heritage.  
**Burgundy** is the courage and alternate identity.

## **12\. Kit System**

### **Home Kits**

**Primary color:** Forest Green  
**Use:** Main match kits

Recommended treatment:

* Forest green jersey  
* Cream collar/placket  
* Subtle burgundy trim  
* Antique gold detail where appropriate  
* Forest green shorts  
* Cream and burgundy striping on cuffs/shorts

  ### **Away Kits**

**Primary color:** Cream / Ivory  
**Use:** Away matches and alternate contrast

Recommended treatment:

* Cream/ivory jersey  
* Forest green collar/placket  
* Forest green shoulder or side trim  
* Subtle burgundy cuff or piping accents  
* Forest green shorts

This is the best away direction: traditional, readable, and still unmistakably SRFC.

### **Heritage / Red Kits**

**Primary color:** Heritage Burgundy  
**Use:** Alternate, tournament, rivalry, founder’s day, or heritage fixtures

Recommended treatment:

* Deep burgundy jersey  
* Cream collar/placket  
* Forest green trim  
* Burgundy shorts  
* Cream/green trim at cuffs and hems

Avoid bright red. The red kit should be **deep burgundy / heritage red**, not modern athletic scarlet.

  ## **14\. Rule of Tincture / Contrast Guidance**

The brand is heraldry-inspired, not bound to strict heraldic law.

Use the rule of tincture as a contrast principle:

* Green on cream works well  
* Burgundy on cream works well  
* Cream on green works well  
* Gold on green works well  
* Gold on cream may need a dark outline  
* Green on burgundy should use a cream or gold outline

For the simplified Hammer & Anvil mark, the green hammer over the burgundy chevron is acceptable because the cream outline improves contrast. For embroidery, thicken the cream outline slightly if needed.

---

## **15\. Typography Direction**

Use classic, readable serif typography.

Avoid:

* Blackletter as a main type treatment  
* Overly medieval fonts  
* Esports fonts  
* Corporate sans-serif blandness  
* Hyper-modern athletic fonts

For the Latin motto:  
**PATIOR UT POTIAR** should be set in small caps, letter-spaced, and readable — not blackletter.

---

## **16\. Visual Style**

The SRFC visual system should be:

* Traditional  
* Shield-based  
* Heraldry-inspired  
* Rugby-club appropriate  
* Vector-friendly  
* Embroidery-friendly  
* Legible at small sizes  
* Restrained and dignified  
* Warm rather than corporate  
* Rugged without being aggressive

Avoid:

* Gradients  
* Chrome effects  
* Aggressive mascot faces  
* Explosions  
* Skulls  
* Crossed weapons  
* Excessive scrollwork  
* Too many symbols in the simplified kit marks
