# Spotsylvania RFC Website Copy Guide

## Core website positioning

Spotsylvania RFC is a rugby football club in formation for Spotsylvania and the surrounding communities. The website should focus on gauging interest, collecting founding-list signups, and explaining the vision clearly.

The site should feel classic, traditional, heritage-minded, local, family-friendly, and serious without being severe.

## Primary audiences

The landing page should speak to:

- Parents of elementary-aged children interested in tag rugby
- Youth players
- Adult players
- Beginners
- Former rugby players
- Coaches
- Referees
- Volunteers
- Sponsors
- Community supporters
- PE teachers and school contacts

## Naming rules for the website

Use **Spotsylvania RFC** as the serious primary identity.

Use **Spotsylvania Rugby** when clarity matters for non-rugby audiences.

Use **Spotsylvania Youth Rugby** for youth-specific sections.

Use **Spotsylvania Tag Rugby** for non-contact/tag rugby sections.

Avoid making **Spotsy Rugby** the main website identity. It may appear only in informal youth/social contexts.

Do not use **SPLV** as a website-facing abbreviation except in future apparel/campaign graphics.

## Youth tag rugby section

### Section title

Youth Tag Rugby

### Body copy

Spotsylvania Youth Rugby is beginning with a simple goal: introduce elementary and middle school students to rugby through safe, non-contact play.

Our first strategy is to work with local PE teachers to bring rugby activities into schools during April and May. Students can learn the basics of the game — running, passing, support, spacing, teamwork, and tag defense — in a familiar school setting.

From there, interested families will be invited to join the founding list for a summer non-contact rugby program or sevens-style tag rugby opportunity.

No experience required. No tackling. Just movement, teamwork, courage, and fun.

## Vision section

### Section label

Why Build It

### Heading

More than a sport.  
A club worth forming.

### Body copy

Rugby has a culture unlike anything else in sport — forged through hard training, honest competition, respect for opponents, and fellowship after the final whistle. The aim is to bring that tradition to Spotsylvania in a way that forms character, welcomes families, and gives local players a badge to earn.

## Pillars

### 01 — All Levels Welcome

No experience necessary. Rugby rewards courage, effort, and teachability as much as skill. We want to hear from anyone willing to learn the game properly.

### 02 — Real Fellowship

The fellowship after the match is part of the game. What begins on the pitch — trust, grit, and shared effort — can become friendship that lasts for decades.

### 03 — A Club for Life

From first practice to coaching the next generation, a good rugby club grows with its members. The goal is something permanent, not a flash in the pan.

### 04 — Rooted Locally

This club should belong to this community. We may compete regionally, but our identity should be shaped by the people and place we call home.

## Interest section

### Section label

The First Test

### Heading

Interest first.  
Then the first meeting.

### Body copy

The first goal is simple: build a direct list of people who want to see Spotsylvania RFC happen. Social media attention helps, but a founding list gives organizers a practical way to plan the first interest meeting, identify possible players, find coaches and volunteers, and start conversations with sponsors.

### Who should join the list?

Prospective players, parents, coaches, referees, volunteers, athletic trainers, local businesses, teachers, and anyone who wants to support rugby in Spotsylvania.

### What happens next?

No commitment is required. Your response helps determine whether there is enough interest to schedule an interest meeting, organize early training, and shape the club’s first season.

## Voice rules

Use:

- We are gauging interest
- In formation
- Founding list
- No commitment required
- Put Your Hand Up
- Help build the club
- Unity, courage, service
- Families, volunteers, supporters
- Non-contact tag rugby
- Youth rugby pathway

Avoid:

- Tryouts
- Elite only
- Join now or miss out
- Dominate
- Warrior language
- Overpromising fixtures, rosters, leagues, insurance, school partnerships, or approvals before they exist
