# Spotsylvania RFC Parent FAQ

## Purpose

This document provides parent-facing FAQ language for Spotsylvania RFC / Spotsylvania Youth Rugby / Spotsylvania Tag Rugby.

It is designed for website FAQ sections, school packets, parent handouts, parent emails, flyers, and introductory clinic materials.

The tone should be clear, calm, safe, practical, and welcoming. Parents should understand that the first youth offering is non-contact tag rugby, not tackle rugby.

## Parent messaging principles

### Lead with clarity

Parents need to understand quickly:

- what the program is
- whether it is contact or non-contact
- what ages it is for
- whether experience is required
- what happens after they join the interest list
- whether the school is responsible for the club
- how they can help

### Preferred parent-facing language

Use:

- non-contact rugby
- tag rugby
- youth rugby
- elementary and middle school students
- movement, passing, spacing, teamwork, and fun
- no tackling
- no experience required
- interest list
- in formation
- if there is enough interest

### Avoid leading with

- tackle rugby
- full contact
- summer 7s without explanation
- U6 / U8 / U10 / U12 terminology as the first explanation
- elite pathway
- tryouts
- formal registration language before the program is ready

## Website FAQ

### What is Spotsylvania RFC?

Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club is a rugby club in formation for Spotsylvania and the surrounding communities. The long-term goal is to build a durable rugby pathway for youth players, families, coaches, volunteers, and eventually adult players.

The first youth focus is non-contact rugby for elementary and middle school students.

### What is Spotsylvania Youth Rugby?

Spotsylvania Youth Rugby is the youth-facing program of Spotsylvania RFC. It is the pathway for elementary, middle school, and eventually high school players to learn and play rugby in an age-appropriate way.

The first step is non-contact tag rugby.

### What is Spotsylvania Tag Rugby?

Spotsylvania Tag Rugby is the non-contact entry point for young players. Instead of tackling, players wear tags or flags, and defenders stop play by pulling a tag.

Tag rugby teaches running, passing, spacing, support, evasion, teamwork, communication, and confidence without tackling or collision-based play.

### Is this tackle rugby?

No. The first youth offering is non-contact tag rugby.

That means:

- no tackling
- no scrums
- no rucks
- no mauls
- no lineouts
- no contact drills

Any future contact rugby would require proper coaching, parent communication, age-appropriate progression, registration, insurance, safety standards, and a suitable competition structure.

### Is rugby safe for kids?

The starting format is non-contact tag rugby. As with any sport or PE activity, there is normal running and movement risk, but the introductory format is designed to be age-appropriate and accessible.

The focus is movement, passing, spacing, teamwork, communication, and fun — not collision.

### Does my child need rugby experience?

No. Most children will be brand new to rugby.

The program is designed for beginners and will start with simple skills and games.

### What ages is this for?

The first focus is elementary and middle school students.

Exact age and grade groups will depend on family interest, school interest, volunteer support, field availability, registration requirements, and safety considerations.

For now, families should join the interest list and indicate their child’s grade or age range.

### Why introduce rugby through schools?

PE class is one of the best ways to introduce rugby because students can try the game in a familiar, low-pressure setting.

A short April or May rugby unit can help students learn basic movement, passing, teamwork, and tag defense. Families whose children enjoy it can then learn about summer opportunities through Spotsylvania Youth Rugby.

### What would a school PE rugby unit look like?

A school introduction would be short, simple, and non-contact.

Students might learn:

- how to carry a rugby ball
- how to pass backward
- how to support a teammate
- how to find space
- how to evade defenders
- how tag defense works
- how to play a small-sided tag rugby game

The school format would not include tackling or contact rugby.

### What is Imagine Rugby?

Imagine Rugby is a youth rugby introduction program designed to help children learn rugby through accessible, non-contact activities.

Spotsylvania RFC may use school-friendly rugby activities and similar resources to help introduce rugby in PE settings. Imagine Rugby is best understood as an educational entry point. Spotsylvania Youth Rugby is the local continuation pathway for interested families.

### What happens after the school PE unit?

Students who enjoy the PE unit can ask their parents to join the Spotsylvania Youth Rugby interest list.

If there is enough interest, volunteer support, field access, and proper approval, Spotsylvania RFC may organize a summer non-contact rugby program or sevens-style tag rugby opportunity.

### What is summer sevens-style rugby?

Rugby sevens is a faster, smaller-sided version of rugby.

For younger beginners, any summer sevens-style opportunity would likely be adapted into a non-contact format using tag rugby rules. The goal would be fun, fitness, teamwork, learning, and age-appropriate play.

### Will this become contact rugby later?

Possibly, but not at the beginning for younger players.

The club’s first priority is to introduce rugby safely through non-contact formats. Contact rugby would only be considered later when age, readiness, coaching, league structure, registration, insurance, and safety systems are in place.

### Is this only for boys?

No. Tag rugby can be introduced to boys and girls.

Initial groups will depend on interest, age, grade, volunteer support, and available competition formats.

### Is this only for students at one school?

No. The long-term goal is to build a broader community rugby club serving Spotsylvania and the surrounding region.

Some early activity may begin through specific school relationships, but Spotsylvania RFC is intended to become a community club, not a single-school program.

### Is the school responsible for the club?

No. A school PE introduction would simply be a way for students to experience rugby in a safe, non-contact format.

Any outside-school programming would be organized separately through Spotsylvania RFC / Spotsylvania Youth Rugby, subject to proper approvals, insurance, field access, volunteer support, and registration requirements.

### What equipment does my child need?

For introductory tag rugby, children generally need athletic clothing, a water bottle, sneakers or cleats appropriate for the playing surface, and weather-appropriate clothing.

Specific equipment requirements will be announced once a program schedule is confirmed.

### Do parents need to volunteer?

Parent help will be important.

A new rugby club needs volunteers for communication, coaching assistance, field setup, registration help, equipment, photography, sponsorship, event support, and parent coordination.

No rugby experience is required for many volunteer roles.

### Will there be adult rugby too?

Possibly. Spotsylvania RFC is also collecting interest from adults as players, coaches, referees, volunteers, sponsors, and supporters.

The first youth effort can grow alongside adult and volunteer interest.

### What happens when I join the founding list?

Joining the founding list does not commit your family to a season.

It helps the club understand:

- how many families are interested
- what ages or grades are represented
- who may be willing to coach or volunteer
- whether a summer program is realistic
- what schools and communities may be interested

Families on the list will receive updates about interest meetings, school outreach, summer opportunities, and next steps.

### How do we get involved?

Put your hand up.

Join the founding list and tell us whether you are interested as a parent, youth player, adult player, coach, volunteer, sponsor, teacher, or supporter.

A club begins when enough people decide to step forward.

## Parent-facing claims guardrails

### Safe claims

Use:

- Spotsylvania RFC is in formation.
- We are gauging interest.
- The first youth focus is non-contact tag rugby.
- We hope to introduce rugby through PE activities in April or May.
- Summer opportunities depend on interest, volunteer support, field access, approval, and proper requirements.
- No experience is required.
- Joining the founding list does not commit your family to a season.

### Avoid claiming before confirmed

Do not claim:

- the club is officially chartered
- the club is fully insured
- coaches are certified
- a school has agreed to participate
- a summer program is guaranteed
- a league is guaranteed
- field access is secured
- Rugby Virginia or USA Rugby has approved the specific program, unless confirmed

### Safe sentence

Spotsylvania RFC is a rugby club in formation. We are gauging interest in non-contact youth rugby and exploring school and community pathways, subject to school approval, insurance requirements, volunteer support, field access, and registration requirements.
