# Spotsylvania RFC Source of Truth

## Current status

This document is the working source of truth for Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club branding, website language, youth rugby positioning, and launch messaging.

## Naming rules

Use **Spotsylvania RFC** as the main serious public identity across the website, crest, kit, sponsor materials, adult-facing communications, and official documents.

Use **Spotsylvania Rugby** when clarity matters for parents, schools, sponsors, local media, or people unfamiliar with “RFC.”

Use **Spotsylvania Youth Rugby** for youth-facing and parent-facing programming.

Use **Spotsylvania Tag Rugby** for the non-contact youth entry point, school PE materials, elementary and middle school outreach, and parent-facing safety messaging.

Use **Spotsy Rugby** sparingly. It may work for youth posts, informal local conversation, hashtags, and parent-friendly social content, but it should not carry the serious adult-facing or sponsor-facing identity.

Use **SRFC** for practical small marks, social avatars, favicons, hats, shorts, watermarks, and compact apparel use.

Use **SPLV** only as a secondary graphic device. It may appear on apparel, posters, internal campaign graphics, sleeve marks, founding collection merchandise, or subtle heritage details. Do not use SPLV as the main public abbreviation.

## Brand foundation

Spotsylvania RFC is a traditional rugby football club in formation, built on unity, courage, service, local pride, and lifelong fellowship.

The club should feel traditional, local, disciplined, welcoming, rugged, virtuous, formational, family-friendly, heritage-minded, and community-rooted.

Avoid anything that feels gimmicky, corporate, flashy, cartoonish, esports-like, overly aggressive, or overtly religious.

## Cannons, foundry, and historical imagery

Cannons should **not** become a core club symbol.

Cannons have local historical associations, but they also introduce avoidable risks: militaristic tone, Civil War associations, political or historical sensitivities, youth/school-facing concerns, and distraction from rugby identity.

If used at all, cannons should remain rare, subtle, and historically contextual. Prefer the foundry / iron / forged layer over cannon imagery.

Emphasize formation, craft, and durability. Do not emphasize weapons, battle, or war.

## Logo system

### Primary crest

Use for website hero, posters, social graphics, sponsor decks, flyers, print materials, banners, and email headers.

Should include Spotsylvania RFC, full-color heritage crest, Est. 2026, modern rugby ball, and oak / heritage elements.

### Kit crest

Use for jerseys, shorts, polos, hats, training gear, embroidery, and small apparel applications.

Should be simplified, with no motto, no date, no tiny acorns, readable at small size, and suitable for embroidery or sublimation.

### Future marks needed

Create both:

1. **SRFC small mark** for practical usage.
2. **SPLV heritage code mark** for optional apparel/campaign usage.

## Youth rugby strategy

The first youth focus is non-contact tag rugby for elementary and middle school students, introduced through local PE classes in April and May and followed by summer play opportunities for interested families.

### School-to-summer funnel

```text
April/May PE rugby unit
↓
Non-contact rugby activities in school
↓
Students and parents hear about local rugby
↓
Founding list / youth interest form
↓
Summer tag rugby or sevens-style non-contact play
↓
Fall planning and club formation
```

Imagine Rugby or similar school-friendly rugby introduction materials should be used as the school entry point, not as the club identity.

Spotsylvania RFC is the long-term club. Spotsylvania Youth Rugby / Spotsylvania Tag Rugby are the local continuation pathways.

## Parent-facing safety language

The starting format is non-contact tag rugby. That means no tackling, scrums, rucks, or mauls. The focus is movement, passing, teamwork, communication, and fun.

## Founding campaign phrase

**Put Your Hand Up** is the preferred founding campaign phrase for broad participation.

It grows out of the landing page line:

> A club begins when enough people decide to put their hand up.

Use **Put Your Hand Up** as a heading or campaign phrase, but pair it with a clear action button such as **Join the Founding List**.

Recommended website section:

```text
Put Your Hand Up

A rugby club is built by the people willing to step forward first. Whether you are a parent, player, coach, referee, sponsor, teacher, or supporter, your interest helps determine what Spotsylvania RFC can become.

[Join the Founding List]
```

Do not use “Put Your Hand Up” where a precise transactional label is needed. For forms, buttons, and navigation, use direct labels such as Join the Founding List, Register Interest, Contact Us, Sponsor the Club, or Volunteer Interest.

## One-sentence brand architecture

Spotsylvania Rugby Football Club, publicly known as Spotsylvania RFC, is a traditional rugby club in formation, beginning with Spotsylvania Youth Rugby and Spotsylvania Tag Rugby as safe non-contact entry points, guided by the motto Unity · Courage · Service, rallied by the founding phrase Put Your Hand Up, and supported by a secondary heritage language of formation, durability, and being Forged in Spotsylvania.
